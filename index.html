The Chemistry of Perfume

Perfume types reflect the concentration of aromatic compounds in a solvent, which in fine fragrance is typically ethanol or a mix of water and ethanol. The intensity and longevity of a fragrance are based on the concentration, intensity, and longevity of the aromatic compounds, or perfume oils, used.

The Chemistry of Connection

The conventional application of pure perfume (parfum extrait) in Western cultures is behind the ears, at the nape of the neck, under the armpits and at the insides of wrists, elbows and knees so that the pulse point will warm the perfume and release fragrance continuously.
